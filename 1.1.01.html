Subject-Verb Agreement

Master one of the most frequently tested grammar rules in SSC CGL English.

What is Subject-Verb Agreement?

Subject-Verb Agreement means the verb in a sentence must agree with its subject in number (singular/plural) and person (1st, 2nd, 3rd).

💡 Golden Rule: A singular subject takes a singular verb; a plural subject takes a plural verb.

Core Rules

Rule 1 – Basic Agreement

When the subject is singular, add -s or -es to the verb.

  • The dog barks loudly. ✓
  • The dogs bark loudly. ✓

Rule 2 – Compound Subjects with "and"

Two subjects joined by and take a plural verb, unless they refer to a single entity.

  • Ram and Shyam are friends. ✓
  • Bread and butter is my breakfast. ✓ (single idea)

Rule 3 – Either/Neither … Or/Nor

The verb agrees with the subject closer to it.

  • Either the students or the teacher is responsible.
  • Neither the manager nor the employees were informed.

Rule 4 – Collective Nouns

Collective nouns (team, jury, committee) take a singular verb when acting as a unit.

  • The jury has reached a verdict. ✓

SSC CGL Tricky Cases

📌 Watch out: Phrases like "along with", "as well as", "together with", "in addition to" do not change the number of the subject. The verb agrees with the first subject only.

Example: The principal, along with all the teachers, was present.
